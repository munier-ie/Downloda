Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 6, 2026

1. Overview

Downloda is built from the ground up to respect user privacy. Unlike typical media downloader utilities, Downloda has no tracking scripts, no advertisements, and requires no account creation. Everything occurs locally on your own Android device, ensuring absolute privacy.

2. Data Collection & Usage

Downloda collects no personal information. Specifically:

  • No Accounts: You do not need to register, sign up, or log in to use Downloda.
  • No Trackers: We do not include any tracking software, telemetry SDKs, analytical tools, or cookies inside the app.
  • No Usage Logging: Your download logs, URL history, and favorite presets are stored exclusively in your local database using Drift (SQLite) on your own device and are never transmitted to us or any external servers.

3. Media Processing & Storage

All media downloads, stream merging (DASH muxing), and conversions (video to MP3 audio encoding) are processed entirely locally on your own device using native processes and FFmpeg.

No video or audio content is uploaded, routed through proxy servers, or stored on external servers under our control. The downloaded media files are written directly into your device's default storage folder (typically /Download/Downloda).

4. System Permissions Used

To perform background media operations natively, Downloda requests the following Android system permissions:

  • INTERNET: Required to retrieve stream metadata and download the media chunks from the content host.
  • FOREGROUND_SERVICE: Required to keep downloads running reliably in the background without interruptions from Android's resource managers.
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS: Required to display the download progress bar, queue status, speed metrics, and completion notifications.
  • Storage/Media Access: Used to write the downloaded files directly to your device’s Download directory.

5. Third-Party Integrations

Downloda directly interfaces with public Content Delivery Networks (CDNs) of social media platforms (e.g., YouTube's googlevideo servers) or your self-hosted API backend (universal_downloader) to parse stream URLs. When you request a download, your device communicates directly with these CDNs. These third-party services may process your IP address and request metadata in accordance with their own respective privacy policies.

6. Open Source

Downloda is fully open-source. Anyone can review the complete codebase on GitHub to verify our security implementation and confirm our zero-tracking, offline-first privacy standards.

7. Contact

If you have any questions or concerns about our privacy standards, feel free to open a discussion or file an issue on our GitHub repository.
