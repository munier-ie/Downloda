Meet the Developer

"I just wanted to download a video and keep scrolling."

It started as a daily annoyance. I'd be scrolling TikTok, YouTube, or X, spot a clip I wanted to save, and have to go through the same tedious chore: tap share, copy link, close my feed, find the downloader app, launch it, wait through a splash ad, paste, and wait.

It was a jarring disruption to a simple flow. Most downloader utilities had become bloated, ad-ridden, and invasive, treating users like telemetry products.

So I decided to solve it. I asked myself: Why can't the download just happen quietly in the background without making me leave the app?

That's why I built Downloda. By integrating directly with Android's system share sheet, Downloda catches the shared URL, spins up a background worker to fetch and merge video and audio streams locally using compiled FFmpeg, and saves the file quietly. No app switching. No copy-pasting. No tracking cookies. You tap share, select Downloda, and keep scrolling. It does exactly what it's supposed to do, and nothing more.
